# Copy System

## Voice

Restrained. Confident. Sharp. Loaded. Every word carries weight. No filler. No hedging. No enthusiasm. The voice of a lab that knows what it has.

## Tone Rules

1. **Minimal** — say less than the reader expects. Let whitespace and interaction do the work.
2. **Declarative** — state facts and positions. Do not ask questions or seek validation.
3. **Premium** — every line should feel intentional, like it was chosen over 50 alternatives.
4. **Specific where it matters** — use real material science vocabulary (piezoelectric, shape-memory, metamaterial) to signal depth, but don't over-explain.
5. **Mysterious where it helps** — some things are better unsaid. Withhold strategically.

## What to Never Write

- "Welcome to Sunder"
- "We're building the future of..."
- "Our mission is to..."
- "Passionate team of..."
- "Leveraging cutting-edge..."
- Any sentence that could appear on 1000 other startup sites
- Exclamation marks
- Emoji

## Copy Inventory

### Hero
- "The future is material."

### Thesis
- "We engineer matter that responds — materials that sense force and reconfigure under signal, heat, or field."
- "From passive substrate to active interface."

### Domain Descriptors
- Robotics: "Compliant structures. Adaptive joints. Materials that move with intent."
- Space: "Thermal metamaterials. Radiation-adaptive composites. Materials that endure."
- Defense: "Impact-responsive armor. Shape-shifting surfaces. Materials that protect."
- Energy: "Piezoelectric substrates. Phase-change composites. Materials that convert."

### Navigation Language
- SUNDER (wordmark, home)
- Research
- Applications
- Access

### CTA Options (Ranked)
1. "Request access" — best for stealth phase. Creates scarcity.
2. "Enter the lab" — strong if we add an immersive entry transition
3. "Begin observation" — lab-like, slightly more niche
4. "Inquire" — formal, appropriate for investor-tier
