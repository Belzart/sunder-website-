# Positioning

## Category

Next-generation materials. Active matter. Responsive substrates. This is a new category — the site should make visitors feel they are encountering something that doesn't have a name yet.

## Positioning Statement (Internal)

Sunder is building programmable matter — materials that sense, respond, and reconfigure. We are currently in stealth, establishing the brand and vision before revealing specific products or science.

## What We Claim

- We work at the frontier of materials science
- We are building something new
- Our material is responsive, adaptive, and programmable
- We serve industries where materials are the bottleneck
- Access is limited and intentional

## What We Do NOT Claim

- Specific product capabilities
- Scientific breakthroughs (yet)
- Partnerships or customers (yet)
- Revenue or traction
- Detailed technical specifications

## Language Strategy

### Use
- Matter, material, substrate, surface, structure
- Respond, reconfigure, adapt, sense, shift
- Force, signal, field, heat, pressure
- Lab, research, interface, specimen, sample
- Access, observe, explore, enter

### Avoid
- "Revolutionizing" / "disrupting" / "game-changing"
- "AI-powered" / "blockchain" / any trend-riding language
- "We're excited to announce"
- "Our team of passionate experts"
- Any Silicon Valley startup clichés
- Fluffy fake science prose
- Buzzword soup

## Competitive Differentiation

We don't have named competitors. Our differentiation is:
1. The site itself — no materials company has a site this interactive
2. The material thesis — active matter is genuinely novel
3. The taste level — everything signals premium, not scrappy

## Stealth Strategy

Being in stealth is a feature, not a limitation. The limited information creates scarcity and intrigue. The CTA is access-gated: you request to be let in. This creates the feeling of an exclusive early-stage opportunity.
